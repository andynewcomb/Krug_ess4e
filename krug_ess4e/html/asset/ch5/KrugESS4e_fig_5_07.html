Figure 5.7: FIGURE 5-7 The Revenue from an Excise Tax
Figure 5.7: The revenue from a $40 excise tax on hotel rooms is $200,000, equal to the tax rate, $40—the size of the wedge that the tax drives between the supply price and the demand price—multiplied by the number of rooms rented, 5,000. This is equal to the area of the shaded rectangle.
